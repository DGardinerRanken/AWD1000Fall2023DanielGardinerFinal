Frequently Asked Questions

Rock paper Scissors is considered to be the oldest hand game in the world. The game dates all the way back to the Chinese Han Dynasty. There are also accounts of the game in Japanese history.


Every year on August 27th. The World Rock Paper Scissors Association in Ottawa, Ontario created this day in 2014. Their goal was to encourage as many people as possible to play the game for fun. World Rock Paper Scissors Day is a way to celebrate the only game, that regardless of language, gender, or age, everyone has a chance to win.

Believe it or not, there are numerous variations of Rock, Paper, Scissors played accross the globe. Some versions include additional elements suck as well, spock, lizard, or water. These variations add depth and complexity to the game, keeping it interesting and challenging.

When people play Rock, Paper, Scissors, their most common instinct is to choose rock. This preference for rock could be due to its perceived strength or its simplicity. So, if you want to increase your chances of winning, it might be wise to try using paper or scissors more often!

In 2010, two competitors in China battled it out in a game of Rock, Paper, Scissors that lasted a mind-boggling 101 hours. Their determination and endurance demonstrated the incredible dedication some players have for this seemingly simple game.
